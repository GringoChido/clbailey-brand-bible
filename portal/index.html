Module 01

Brand Foundation — The Strategic Anchor

We do not compete on the showroom floor. We compete on the timeline of a home.

The Three Pillars

I · Craftsmanship as Proof
Solid hardwood, 1-inch diamond-honed slate, 8 hand-applied finishes. A spec sheet, not an adjective.
II · Structural Endurance
Built for residential longevity measured in decades. Re-levelable. Handed down, not hauled off.
III · Multi-Functional Space
Game night to dinner party. Convertible dining tops and matched-family collections.

The Three Personas

The Discerning Homeowner
"Will this look like a bar, or like furniture?" Win with: Furniture first. Game table second.
The Legacy Family
"Is this a purchase or an investment?" Win with: Buy it once. Pass it down.
The Dealer Principal
"Will this protect my margin and not blow up in a claim?" Win with: Sell it with confidence.

Positioning statement. C.L. Bailey & Co. sits at the intersection of premium residential furniture design and championship-level play performance — solid hardwood on 1-inch diamond-honed slate, finished by hand in 8 coats, engineered to be inherited. We are the table that is still in the room a generation later.

Module 02

Visual Identity — The Creative Assets

Three marks, three jobs. They are not interchangeable — choosing the wrong mark for the context is a brand violation, not a style preference.

Logo Hierarchy

The Primary Mark — the heritage badge. Black filigree frame, Deep Heritage Emerald lettering, Tomball, Texas ribbon. Never recolored, stretched, outlined, or rebuilt. On Emerald, Iron, or wood grounds it sits on a Bone plate — the mark itself is never reversed.

1 · Primary Mark
The full ornamental badge with the Tomball, Texas ribbon. Heroes, covers, storefronts. Must appear at largest hierarchy on every first-impression surface.
2 · Secondary Lockup
Crest + wordmark on one baseline, built for short, wide spaces: site headers, email signatures, co-op ad footers, nav bars.
3 · Submark / Crest
Interlocked "CLB" monogram. Favicon, embroidery, social avatar, and the cast brass rail nameplate. Must survive as a single-color silhouette at 16px.

Exclusion Zone & Minimum Sizes

MarkDigital minPrint / physical min
Primary Wordmark180px wide1.25 in (32mm)
Secondary Lockup120px wide0.875 in (22mm)
Crest (screen)16px, single color—
Crest — brass nameplate—1.5 in, 1.0mm relief
Crest — embroidery—2.0 in, 12mm stitch detail

Never: recolor, stretch, outline, or rebuild a mark. Below the Primary minimum, switch to the Secondary Lockup — do not shrink the Primary.

Imagery Direction

✓ Always

  • High-end residential photography in warm, authentic ambient light — golden-hour, lamp-lit, hearth-lit.
  • Real, lived-in interiors; families using the furniture.
  • Wood grain and finish legible; one image per collection with the convertible dining top deployed.
  • Warm grade (4800–5200K feel), protected shadows, charcoal blacks — never crushed to #000.

— Never

  • Clinical white-background catalog cutouts or cold studio strobe.
  • Empty rooms with no human warmth; over-saturated "showroom" lighting.
  • Any frame where the table reads as a bar fixture rather than furniture.

Module 03

Editorial Identity — The Copy & Voice

Timeless, honest, authoritative — yet approachable, like a master carpenter explaining their work across the bench. We state facts plainly and let the material do the bragging. We never oversell, because the spec sheet already won.

The "This, Not That" Matrix

We are……but notIn practice
PremiumElitist"Built to be used by your whole family," not "for the discerning few."
TraditionalAntiquated"Time-tested joinery," not "old-fashioned."
DurableIndustrial"Engineered to be re-leveled and inherited," not "heavy-duty contractor-grade."
ConfidentArrogant"The slate is one inch. Here's why that matters," not "the best table money can buy."
WarmFolksy"Made by hand in Tomball," not "good ol' Texas craftsmanship, y'all."
DetailedJargon-heavyExplain the diamond-honing; don't hide behind it.
AspirationalPretentious"The room your family gathers in," not "an objet d'art for the connoisseur."

Mechanical Rules — Absolute

Oxford comma
Required, always. "Solid hardwood, diamond-honed slate, and hand-applied finish."
Exclamation points
Maximum one per dealer announcement; zero in product, spec, or warranty copy.
Numerals
Spell one–nine in prose; figures for 10+ and always for measurements, prices, and specs: "1-inch slate," "8 finishes," "$4,295 MSRP."
Prices
MSRP with dollar sign and comma separators. "$3,995," never "under $4k."
Em dash
With spaces around it — the brand voice favors the considered pause.
Capitalization
Collection names are proper nouns: Skylar, Viking, Tunbridge. "Diamond-Honed Slate" title-case on first use only.

Brand Lexicon

✓ Approved — use exactly

Solid Hardwood Only Diamond-Honed Slate Hand-Applied Finish 8 Signature Finishes Matched-Family Collection Convertible Dining Top Heirloom Re-levelable Lifetime Structural Guarantee Authorized Dealer Game Room Furniture Handcrafted in Tomball, Texas

— Banned — never

MDF* Particle Board Veneer Substrate Cheap Budget Affordable "Pool table" as lead noun Game Room Toys Bar Man Cave Unit Disposable

*The one sanctioned use of "MDF" — the brand's most powerful sentence:
"Most tables in this price range hide MDF or particle board under a veneer. We don't. Solid hardwood, all the way through — because an heirloom can't be hollow."
Permitted in dealer training, showroom murals, and comparison copy. Factually exact, never exaggerated.

Module 06

Paid Media & Direct Response

Co-op rules that protect the heirloom positioning and the dealer network. Headlines lead with the spec or the heirloom promise — never a discount.

✓ Must

  • Label every price as MSRP.
  • CTA is "Find a Dealer" — we are dealer-distributed.
  • PMax asset groups: Secondary Lockup (never crest-only), one residential hero, one craft macro, one convertible-top demo.
  • Meta video: first 3 seconds show the finished room, not the logo.
  • Guarantee Brass seal bottom-right, ≥0.5 in, with ® and terms footnote.
  • Co-op ads carry the dealer's name in an approved co-brand lockup (Module 09).

— Never

  • Below-MSRP figures or strikethrough "was/now" pricing in brand or co-op creative.
  • "Shop Now."
  • Sitewide percentage offers in brand creative — only "Available at Authorized Dealers" / "In-Store Exclusive."
  • Image text over 20% of frame (PMax).
Required legal micro-line (≥11px / 6pt)
C.L. Bailey & Co. products sold through Authorized Dealers. MSRP shown;
dealer pricing may vary. Lifetime Structural Guarantee subject to terms
at clbailey.com/guarantee.

Module 08

AI Operations & Prompt Engineering

The brand voice, encoded. Prepend this system prompt to any AI copy task — it is the enforceable version of Modules 1 and 3.

Copy-and-paste system prompt
You are the copywriter for C.L. Bailey & Co., a handcrafted game room
furniture brand established in Tomball, Texas in 1999. Your tagline is
"Built for Homes That Endure."

VOICE: Write like a master carpenter explaining their work across the
bench — timeless, honest, authoritative, and approachable. State facts
plainly and let the material brag for you. Never oversell; the spec
sheet already won the argument.

NON-NEGOTIABLE FACTS (keep exact):
- Solid hardwood only — never MDF or particle board.
- 1-inch diamond-honed slate.
- 8 signature hand-applied multi-coat finishes.
- Matched-family collections (Skylar, Viking, Tunbridge).
- Dual-purpose convertible dining/buffet tops.
- Lifetime Structural Guarantee; re-levelable; built to be inherited.
- Sold through Authorized Dealers (CTA is always "Find a Dealer").

MECHANICS: Oxford comma always. Maximum ONE exclamation point per
piece, zero in spec or warranty copy. Spell out one–nine; figures for
10+ and always for measurements and prices. Capitalize collection names.

POSITIONING: premium not elitist · traditional not antiquated · durable
not industrial · confident not arrogant · warm not folksy.

BANNED WORDS: MDF/particle board (except the sanctioned "No MDF"
contrast claim), veneer substrate, cheap, budget, affordable,
"man cave," "bar," "game room toys," "unit," disposable.

If a requested claim is not in the facts above, do not invent it — ask
for the spec or omit it.

AI-Cliché Ban List

delve testament revolutionize seamless(ly) elevate unlock game-changer tapestry bustling in today's fast-paced world look no further nestled whether you're… or… it's worth noting embark navigate (figurative) robust synergy curated boasts

Append to any AI request: "Do not use any of the banned AI-cliché words. Write plainly, as a craftsperson would speak."

Image Generation Guardrails

Negative prompt — always include
--no plastic, glossy plastic finish, warped perspective, melting wood,
extra legs, floating table, impossible proportions, distorted pockets,
oversaturated, white seamless studio background, cartoon, CGI sheen, fisheye

Brand truth check. Always specify real dimensions (88×50 in footprint for an 8-foot table), natural wood grain, matte hand-applied finish, and warm lamp-lit residential interiors. AI imagery is for concepting and mood only — it MUST NOT be passed off as a photograph of actual product in dealer-facing or paid assets.

Module 09

Co-Branding & Brand Interoperability

When a retailer features our mark beside its own, these rules protect trademark integrity. Binding on every co-op and co-branded asset.

✓ Must

  • Retain ™/® on the C.L. Bailey mark, unaltered — never recolored, stretched, or restyled to "match" the retailer.
  • Place our mark only on Bone, Emerald, Iron, or an approved wood field.
  • Carry the attribution micro-line: "C.L. Bailey & Co. is a registered mark. Featured by [Retailer], an Authorized Dealer."

— Never

  • Let either mark intrude on the other's exclusion zone.
  • Reduce our mark below the 85% floor or the §2.3 minimums — even in dealer-funded ads.
  • Permit "official," "exclusive," or "partner" language without written brand-team approval.

Module 10

Photography & Image Governance — The Image Standard

Extends §2.6 imagery direction, §2.7 naming, and §8.3 AI parameters into one governing standard for every image on every surface — web, social, paid, dealer portal, print.

The one-sentence standard. Every C.L. Bailey photograph is a warm, lamp-lit room in one believable Texas home, where solid hardwood furniture is being lived with — and the felt is emerald.

Light, Color & the Felt Law

✓ Always

  • Deep Heritage Emerald is the default playing surface in all brand photography. A second approved felt appears only when the felt program itself is the subject.
  • Warm ambient light — golden-hour, lamp-lit, hearth-lit. Practical sources visible where natural.
  • Warm grade, 4800–5200K feel. Protected shadows — moody is correct, crushed is not. Charcoal blacks, never #000.
  • Rooms dressed inside the brand system: emerald, bone, iron, the three woods, brass and slate accents.

— Never

  • Burgundy, blue, purple, black, or tan felts as set dressing — retired from brand imagery.
  • Cold strobe, midday flat-bright, or LED-strip "games room" lighting.
  • Saturation pushes; off-system prop colors as a dominant field.
  • Skylines that aren't Texan; white-box lofts; anywhere-and-nowhere rooms.

One Collection, One House

Each collection lives in one architectural world, documented in a one-page room bible before any shoot or generation. A new world requires the same written approval as a new brand color.

Skylar — the modern emerald library
Clean-lined contemporary home; emerald millwork; brass and linen; morning-to-lamplight range.
Viking — the Texas hill-country lodge
Stone, timber, forged iron; big hearth; dusk and firelight. Replaces the beach-house and Manhattan-loft worlds.
Tunbridge — the transitional family home
Warm whites, weathered brown, generational mix; Sunday-afternoon light.

Non-conforming files are renamed or purged on the monthly audit.

The Publish Gate — No Image Ships Until Every Row Passes

#Gate
1Maps to exactly one shot type in the taxonomy.
2Felt is emerald — or the image is an approved felt-program asset.
3Light and grade meet the standard: warm, protected shadows, true tones.
4Setting belongs to the collection's documented world.
5Wood grain legible; proportions true; pockets, legs, and rails anatomically correct.
6If generated: Tier permitted, product-true, named human sign-off, Gen source tag.
7Filename conforms; no prompt text anywhere in URL, alt, or metadata.

9Correct transform size for the slot; low-quality placeholder present.

Remediation order. This week: rename every prompt-text file in the image library — that is exposure, not polish. Next 30 days: retire off-felt and off-world imagery from primary surfaces; write the three room bibles. Next quarter: shoot the one thing AI cannot do — two days in the Tomball workshop, plus one real Conversion Demo sequence per collection. That is the proof layer no competitor can copy.
