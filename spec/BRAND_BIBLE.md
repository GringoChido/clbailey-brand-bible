# MODULE 1 — BRAND FOUNDATION
### The Strategic Anchor

Everything downstream — every finish ring, every dealer email, every Performance Max headline — resolves back to this module. If a creative decision cannot be traced to a pillar here, it is off-brand by definition.

## 1.1 Vision

> To be the last game room furniture a family ever needs to buy — the heirloom centerpiece that outlives the mortgage, hosts three generations, and is handed down rather than hauled off.

We do not compete on the showroom floor. We compete on the **timeline of a home**. A C.L. Bailey table is engineered to be in the room in 2055.

## 1.2 Mission

To design, build, and finish solid-hardwood game room furniture — entirely free of particle board and MDF — that performs at championship standards for play and integrates as true residential furniture for living. We arm an authorized dealer network with the margin security, material proof, and brand assets to sell that promise with total confidence.

## 1.3 Core Pillars

The brand stands on three load-bearing columns. Like the legs of a slate-bed table, removing any one collapses the structure.

### Pillar I — Craftsmanship as Proof, Not Poetry
We earn the word "handcrafted" with verifiable facts: solid hardwood throughout, **1-inch diamond-honed slate**, and **8 signature hand-applied multi-coat finishes**. Craftsmanship is a spec sheet, not an adjective. Every claim must survive a tape measure and a moisture meter.

### Pillar II — Structural Endurance
We build for **residential longevity** measured in decades. The enemy is not a competitor — it is humidity, weight, leveling drift, and the landfill. Cast-iron-grade durability, kiln-dried hardwood, and re-levelable construction are the substance behind "Built for Homes That Endure."

### Pillar III — Multi-Functional Space Utilization
A C.L. Bailey piece earns its footprint twice. Game night to dinner party in one piece of furniture — **dual-purpose convertible dining/buffet tops** and **matched-family collections** (Skylar, Viking, Tunbridge) that bring complete room harmony. We sell *rooms that work*, not objects that sit.

## 1.4 Target Personas

Three people fund this brand. Every asset must speak to at least one without alienating the other two.

### Persona A — The Discerning Homeowner
- **Who:** Design-literate, 38–58, renovating or finishing a dedicated room. Reads shelter magazines; has a designer or strong opinions.
- **Core anxiety:** "Will this look like a *bar*, or like *furniture*?"
- **What they need to see:** Finish-matched collections, real interiors, fabric/wood pairings, the convertible dining top that hides the felt for a dinner party.
- **Winning message:** *Furniture first. Game table second. No compromise on either.*

### Persona B — The Legacy Family
- **Who:** Multi-generational household, 40–65, investing in a centerpiece for holidays and milestones. Buying memory, not merchandise.
- **Core anxiety:** "Is this a *purchase* or an *investment* my kids will fight over someday — in a good way?"
- **What they need to see:** Longevity proof, the Lifetime Structural Guarantee, the Owner's Care Kit, the "handed down, not hauled off" narrative.
- **Winning message:** *Buy it once. Pass it down.*

### Persona C — The Authorized Dealer Principal
- **Who:** Showroom owner, regional distributor, or floor manager whose income depends on turns and margin.
- **Core anxiety:** "Will this protect my margin, ship reliably, and not blow up in a warranty claim after I've vouched for it?"
- **What they need to see:** MSRP discipline, bulletproof material durability (the No-MDF promise as a *selling weapon*), reliable distribution, co-op ad support, and a portal that makes them look professional to *their* customer.
- **Winning message:** *Sell it with confidence. We've already won the durability argument for you.*

## 1.5 Brand Positioning Statement

> **For** discerning homeowners and the dealers who serve them, **C.L. Bailey & Co.** is the handcrafted game room furniture brand that sits at the unique intersection of **premium residential furniture design** and **championship-level play performance** — because unlike mass-market builders who hide MDF behind veneer and chase a price point, we build entirely from solid hardwood on 1-inch diamond-honed slate, finish by hand in 8 signature coats, and engineer every piece to be re-leveled, restored, and inherited. We are the table that is still in the room a generation later.

**The intersection, stated as a metric promise:** Tournament-grade flatness (1-inch diamond-honed slate, leveled to play spec) *and* dining-room-grade finish (8 hand-applied coats, furniture-matched collections) in a single, convertible piece. Most brands choose one axis. We refuse the trade-off.

# MODULE 2 — VISUAL IDENTITY
### The Creative Assets

## 2.1 Logo Hierarchy

Three marks, three jobs. They are not interchangeable. Choosing the wrong mark for the context is a brand violation, not a style preference.

### 2.1.1 Primary Mark — The Classic Serif Wordmark
- **Form:** "C.L. Bailey & Co." set in the primary editorial serif (see §2.5), letter-spaced for gravitas, with the ampersand as a deliberate calligraphic feature.
- **Use:** Hero placements, page 1 of any document, showroom hero signage, the home page, the cover of every catalog.
- **Rule:** The Primary Mark **MUST** appear at least once, at the largest hierarchy, on any first impression surface (cover, hero, storefront).

### 2.1.2 Secondary Mark — Horizontal Lockup
- **Form:** "C.L. BAILEY & CO." wordmark locked to the right of the crest/submark, on a single baseline, optimized for short, wide spaces.
- **Use:** Website headers, email signatures, dealer co-op ad footers, document running headers, navigation bars.
- **Rule:** Use whenever vertical height is constrained below the Primary Mark's minimum (see §2.3) but horizontal room exists.

### 2.1.3 Submark / Crest — The Favicon, Embroidery & Cast Nameplate
- **Form:** A simplified monogram crest — interlocked "CLB" or the standalone ampersand-and-rule device — designed to survive reduction to a 16px favicon, a stitched apparel emblem, and a **cast brass nameplate** mounted on the table rail.
- **Use:** Favicon, app icon, apparel embroidery, social avatar, the physical brass plate on every table, hang-tag seal.
- **Rule:** The Submark **MUST** remain legible as a single-color silhouette. No gradients, no fine interior detail thinner than the stroke that survives a 12mm embroidery stitch or a 1.0mm cast relief.

## 2.2 Exclusion Zone (Clear Space)

Clear space is measured in units of **"C"** — the cap height of the leading "C" in the logotype.

- **Minimum clear space on all four sides:** `1 × C` for digital, `1.5 × C` for print and physical signage.
- **Nothing** — no text, rule, image edge, fold, or trim — **MAY** intrude into the exclusion zone.
- For the Submark/Crest, the exclusion zone equals **50% of the crest's total height** on every side.

```
   ┌─────────────────────────────────┐
   │            ↑ 1C                  │
   │   ┌─────────────────────────┐    │
   │←1C│   C.L. Bailey & Co.      │1C→ │
   │   └─────────────────────────┘    │
   │            ↓ 1C                  │
   └─────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 2.5 Typography

A two-typeface system, plus a hard-coded fallback stack for environments we don't control (dealer email clients).

### 2.5.1 Editorial Serif — Headlines (H1 / H2)
- **Role:** Display, headlines, the wordmark, pull quotes, the "voice of the master carpenter."
- **Specification:** A high-contrast transitional/old-style serif (reference targets: *Canela*, *Tiempos Headline*, or *Freight Display*). The brand standard family is documented in the asset vault; substitution requires approval.
- **Rules:** Tracking `+10` to `+40` for the wordmark only; H1/H2 set near-normal tracking. Never set serif headlines below 24px on screen.

### 2.5.2 Geometric Sans-Serif — Body & UI
- **Role:** Body copy, captions, UI, tables, dealer-facing data, web. Optimized for readability.
- **Specification:** A clean neo-grotesque/geometric sans (reference target: **Inter**, with Helvetica Neue / Arial as the universal proxy).
- **Rules:** Body 16–18px / line-height 1.55–1.65. Minimum body size 14px. Never set body in a wood-tone color.

### 2.5.3 Type Scale (modular, ratio ≈ 1.25)

| Token | Size (px) | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Display | 56 | Hero only |
| H1 | 40 | Page title (serif) |
| H2 | 30 | Section (serif) |
| H3 | 22 | Subsection (sans, semibold) |
| Body-L | 18 | Lead paragraph |
| Body | 16 | Default |

### 2.5.4 Universal Fallback Stack (dealer email & uncontrolled environments)

```css

/* Body / UI */

```

Dealer email templates **MUST** use the fallback stack — never web-font-only declarations — because outbound dealer mail renders in Outlook, Apple Mail, and Gmail without our hosted fonts.

## 2.6 Imagery Direction

- **Always:** High-end residential photography. Warm, authentic ambient light (golden-hour, lamp-lit, hearth-lit). Real, lived-in interior spaces. Families *using* the furniture — a dinner served on the convertible top, a multi-generation game night, a quiet morning with the room at rest.
- **Never:** Clinical white-background "catalog cutout" stock. Cold studio strobe. Empty rooms with no human warmth. Over-saturated "showroom" lighting. Any image where the table reads as a *bar fixture* rather than *furniture*.
- **Composition:** Wood grain and finish must be legible. At least one image per collection must show the convertible dining top *deployed* (felt hidden) to prove the dual-purpose claim.
- **Color grade:** Warm white balance (4800–5200K feel), protected shadows, emerald and wood tones true-to-finish. Never crush blacks to pure `#000`; honor the Cast Iron charcoal.

# MODULE 3 — EDITORIAL IDENTITY
### The Copy & Voice

## 3.1 Tone

Timeless, honest, authoritative — yet approachable, like a master carpenter explaining their work across the bench. We state facts plainly and let the material do the bragging. We never oversell, because the spec sheet already won. We respect the reader's intelligence and their money.

The voice is the voice of someone who has built the thing with their hands, knows exactly why the slate is one inch and not three-quarters, and will tell you — without condescension — what that means for the next thirty years of your home.

## 3.2 The "This, Not That" Copywriting Matrix

| We Are… | …But NOT | In Practice |
|---|---|---|
| **Premium** | Elitist | "Built to be used by your whole family," not "for the discerning few." |
| **Traditional** | Antiquated | "Time-tested joinery," not "old-fashioned." We honor heritage without smelling of mothballs. |
| **Durable** | Industrial | "Engineered to be re-leveled and inherited," not "heavy-duty contractor-grade." Furniture, not equipment. |
| **Confident** | Arrogant | "The slate is one inch. Here's why that matters," not "the best table money can buy." |
| **Warm** | Folksy/Cutesy | "Made by hand in Tomball," not "good ol' Texas craftsmanship, y'all." |
| **Detailed** | Jargon-heavy | Explain the diamond-honing; don't hide behind it. |
| **Aspirational** | Pretentious | "The room your family gathers in," not "an objet d'art for the connoisseur." |

## 3.3 Mechanical Rules — Punctuation & Numerics (Absolute)

- **Oxford comma:** REQUIRED, always. "Solid hardwood, diamond-honed slate, and hand-applied finish."
- **Exclamation points:** MAXIMUM **one (1)** per dealer announcement; **zero** in product copy, spec language, or warranty text. Enthusiasm is shown through the fact, not the punctuation.
- **Em dash:** Use the em dash (—) with spaces around it for asides. The brand voice favors the considered pause.
- **Numerals:** Spell out one through nine in prose; use figures for 10 and above, and ALWAYS use figures for measurements, prices, and specs ("1-inch slate," "8 finishes," "$4,295 MSRP"). Never "one-inch slate" in spec contexts.
- **Measurements:** Always hyphenate compound modifiers: "1-inch diamond-honed slate," "3/4-inch" only when quoting an inferior competitor spec for contrast.
- **Prices:** Always show MSRP with a dollar sign and comma separators; never round-trick ("$3,995," never "under $4k").
- **Capitalization:** Collection names are proper nouns — always capitalized (Skylar, Viking, Tunbridge). "Diamond-Honed Slate" is title-case as a proprietary feature term on first use, lowercase thereafter.

## 3.4 Brand Lexicon

### ✅ Approved Terms (use these exactly)
- **Solid Hardwood Only**
- **Diamond-Honed Slate** (1-inch)
- **Hand-Applied Finish** / **8 Signature Finishes**
- **Matched-Family Collection** / **Room Harmony**
- **Convertible Dining Top** / **Dual-Purpose**
- **Heirloom** / **Built for Homes That Endure**
- **Re-levelable** / **Residential Longevity**
- **Lifetime Structural Guarantee**
- **Authorized Dealer**
- **Game Room Furniture** (the category we own)
- **Handcrafted in Tomball, Texas (est. 1999)**

### ⛔ Banned Terms (never, under any circumstance)
- **MDF** / **Medium-Density Fiberboard** (except in the explicit "No MDF" contrast claim)
- **Particle Board**
- **Veneer Substrate** / "veneered" (implies hollow construction)
- **Cheap** / **Budget** / **Affordable** (we are *value*, never *cheap*)
- **Pool Table** *as the lead noun* — we lead with "game room furniture"; "pool table" is acceptable secondarily
- **Game Room Toys** / **Toys** / **Gear**
- **Bar** (as in "looks great in your man-cave bar")
- **Man Cave** (dated, gendered, off-positioning)
- **Unit** (it is a *piece* or a *table*, never a "unit")
- **Disposable** / **Replace**

### The "No MDF" Contrast Claim (the one sanctioned use of the banned word)
> "Most tables in this price range hide MDF or particle board under a veneer. We don't. Solid hardwood, all the way through — because a heirloom can't be hollow."

This claim is the brand's single most powerful sentence. It MAY appear in dealer training, showroom murals, and comparison copy. It MUST be factually exact and never exaggerated.

# MODULE 4 — DIGITAL MARKETING & SOCIAL MEDIA

## 4.1 Social Content Grid & Safe Zones

- **Feed grid:** A rolling 9-tile rhythm of **3 content types**: (1) Finished-room hero, (2) Craft/material detail macro (grain, slate edge, finish coat), (3) Family-in-use lifestyle. Never two of the same type adjacent.
- **Aspect ratios:** Feed `4:5` (1080×1350). Reels/Shorts/TikTok `9:16` (1080×1920). Story `9:16`.
- **Reel / Short safe title zone:** Keep all critical text and logo within the **center 80% vertically** and **clear of the top 220px and bottom 420px** (UI, captions, CTA buttons live there). Title text never within 64px of any edge.
- **Lower-thirds (video):** Cast Iron charcoal bar at 85% opacity, Slate Silver hairline top border (2px), serif name + sans descriptor. Position bottom, above the 420px UI safe zone. Collection name in serif, spec in sans.
- **Closed captions (hard-coded):** Inter Semibold, 42–48px at 1080-wide, Bone/Ivory text (`#F4F1EA`) with a Cast Iron charcoal box at 70% opacity. Two lines maximum. This matches the typography module exactly — no platform auto-caption fonts.

## 4.2 Social Caption Blueprint

Every caption follows the three-beat structure. No exceptions.

```
[ HOOK ]          — 1 line. A fact or a question. No emoji in the hook.
                    e.g. "The slate is one inch thick. Most aren't."

[ CORE NARRATIVE ]— 2–4 lines. The craft, the why, the room. Master-carpenter voice.
                    Approved lexicon only. Oxford commas. Max one exclamation, total.

[ FIND A DEALER CTA ] — 1 line + link.
                    e.g. "Find the Skylar at an authorized dealer near you → link in bio."
```

### Emoji Parameters (strict)
- **Maximum 2 emoji per caption**, never in the hook, never mid-sentence.
- **Approved set only:** 🎱 (sparingly), 🪵, 🏡, ✨ (max once). 
- **Banned:** 🔥, 💰, 🤑, 😍, 👇 (and all "hype" emoji). We are premium, not a clearance sale.

### Official Hashtag Groupings
- **Brand core (always):** `#CLBaileyCo` `#BuiltToEndure`
- **Category:** `#BilliardsDesign` `#GameRoomFurniture` `#HeirloomFurniture`
- **Craft:** `#SolidHardwood` `#HandAppliedFinish` `#DiamondHonedSlate`
- **Collection (when relevant):** `#CLBSkylar` `#CLBViking` `#CLBTunbridge`
- **Rule:** 6–10 hashtags total. Always lead with the two brand-core tags. Never more than 10 (reads as spam, off-brand).

# MODULE 5 — ENVIRONMENTAL & PHYSICAL TOUCHPOINTS

## 5.1 Showroom & Dealer Floor Branding

- **Overhead hanging signage:** Suspended Cast Iron charcoal panel, Primary Wordmark in Bone/Ivory, Slate Silver underline rule. Minimum mounting height 8 ft to bottom edge; minimum panel width 36 in. Exclusion zone `1.5 × C`.
- **Dimensional wall logo:** Crest in cast-brass-finish or routed solid hardwood, minimum 18 in tall, mounted on an Emerald or Espresso accent wall. Stand-off depth 1–1.5 in to cast a true shadow (depth signals substance).
- **The "No MDF" Promise Wall Mural:** A required fixture for flagship dealers. Cutaway graphic of a C.L. Bailey rail showing solid hardwood vs. a competitor's MDF core, with the sanctioned contrast claim (§3.4) set in serif headline. Minimum 48 in × 72 in.
- **Finish-Sample Rings:** The **8 standard wood-finish samples** presented on a hardwood ring or rail, each sample minimum 3 in × 4 in, edge-finished to show the full multi-coat depth, labeled with finish name in sans + the approved finish lexicon. The ring is a hero merchandising tool, not a back-office binder.

## 5.2 Point of Sale (POS) & Point of Purchase (POP)

- **Table-tent cards (on the felt):** Placed directly on the playing surface of a display *Skylar* or *Viking*. Footprint max 5 in × 7 in folded, felt-safe weighted base (no scratch). Front: collection name (serif) + one hero spec. Inside: convertible dining-top callout + finish options. Bone/Ivory stock, Emerald and Brass print, soft-touch matte.
- **Product feature hang-tags:** Attached to the rail. 2.5 in × 5 in, Crest at top, three approved-lexicon spec bullets (Solid Hardwood Only · 1-inch Diamond-Honed Slate · Hand-Applied Finish), MSRP on reverse. Brass foil seal on the Lifetime Structural Guarantee line.
- **Pricing matrices:** Tabular, by collection, with the convertible dining-top option shown as a distinct line — never buried. MSRP discipline per Module 6. Slate Silver header row, Emerald collection labels.

## 5.3 Packaging & Delivery Assets

- **Corner guards:** Heavy-duty double-wall protective cardboard corner guards, Cast Iron charcoal with Crest in single-color Bone. Min 4 in leg length, rated to protect a leveled slate edge in transit.

## 8.3 Generative Image Prompt Parameters (Midjourney / Stable Diffusion)

To prevent AI wood furniture from rendering plasticky, warped, or impossibly scaled:

- **Always specify:** "solid hardwood pool table, visible natural wood grain, matte hand-applied finish, realistic 1-inch slate-bed proportions, photographed in a warm lamp-lit residential interior, golden-hour ambient light, shallow depth of field, editorial furniture photography, true-to-scale dimensions (88in x 50in footprint for an 8-foot table)."
- **Material realism anchors:** "natural wood grain continuity, no plastic sheen, no glossy lacquer reflections, accurate leg-to-rail joinery, level slate playing surface."
- **Negative prompt (always include):** `--no plastic, glossy plastic finish, warped perspective, melting wood, extra legs, floating table, impossible proportions, distorted pockets, oversaturated, white seamless studio background, cartoon, CGI sheen, fisheye`
- **Aspect / params:** `--ar 4:5` (feed) or `--ar 16:9` (web hero), `--style raw` for photographic realism.
- **Scale guardrail:** Always state real dimensions in the prompt so the model anchors proportions; never let the model guess table size. Reject any output where pockets, rails, or leg counts are anatomically wrong — these are non-shippable.
- **Brand truth check:** AI imagery is for *concepting and mood only* until a human verifies it reads as furniture, not equipment, and shows true wood/finish. AI images MUST NOT be passed off as photographs of actual product in dealer-facing or paid assets.

# MODULE 9 — CO-BRANDING & BRAND INTEROPERABILITY

When an authorized retail store (e.g., "Billiard Factory & Home Game Rooms") features the C.L. Bailey mark beside its own, the following protect corporate trademark integrity. These rules are binding on every co-op and co-branded asset.

## 9.1 Logo Size Ratio
- In a co-brand lockup, the C.L. Bailey Secondary Lockup and the retailer's mark sit at **equal optical weight** — neither dominates. As a manufacturer-brand, C.L. Bailey's mark is **never smaller than 85%** of the retailer's mark's optical height, and **never larger than 115%**. Parity, with a slight bias to the maker.
- In dealer-funded (non-co-op) advertising, the dealer mark MAY lead, but the C.L. Bailey mark MUST remain at minimum reproduction size (§2.3) and never reduced below the retailer's mark by more than the 85% floor.

## 9.2 Background Isolation
- The two marks MUST be separated by a **vertical Slate Silver divider rule** (1–2px digital / 0.5pt print) OR a clear gap of **at least `2 × C`**.
- Neither mark may sit on a background that fails its color/contrast pairing (§7.2). The C.L. Bailey mark MUST sit on Bone, Emerald, Iron, or an approved wood field — never on a clashing third-party brand color.
- No mark may be placed inside the other's exclusion zone.

## 9.3 Text Alignment & Trademark Integrity
- Co-brand lockups align on a **shared baseline** (horizontal) or **shared center axis** (stacked). No diagonal, arched, or overlapping arrangements.
- The C.L. Bailey mark MUST retain its trademark/registered mark (™/®) and MUST NOT be recolored, restretched, outlined, or altered to "match" the retailer's style.
- Required attribution line beneath any co-brand lockup (micro type): *"C.L. Bailey & Co. is a registered mark. Featured by [Retailer], an Authorized Dealer."*
- A retailer MAY NOT imply manufacture, partnership beyond authorized resale, or exclusive rights. Any "official," "exclusive," or "partner" language requires written approval from the C.L. Bailey brand team.
